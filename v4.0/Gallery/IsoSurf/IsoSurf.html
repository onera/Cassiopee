Post : Iso Surfaces

By C. Benoit and S. Peron

Isosurfaces are generated using the Marching-Cubes or Marching-Tetras algorithm.
When starting from an hexa mesh, marching cube can be used to generate a TRI mesh or a QUAD dominant mesh.
QUAD dominant isosurfaces look smoother and have less elements.

In Cassiopee, computing an isosurface of field 'Field' for value 12. by marching tetras is performed by: iso = P.isoSurf(A, 'Field', 12.).
Computing an isosurface of field 'Field' for value 12. by marching cubes: iso = P.isoSurfMC(A, 'Field', 12.).
Iso surface of Q crtieria for a slat extracted with Marching tetra (1.9 M elements) with Marching cube (0.8 M elements)
